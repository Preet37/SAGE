# Card: GNMT (Wu et al., 2016) — Deep LSTM seq2seq fixes for bottlenecks
**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.08144  
**Role:** paper | **Need:** COMPARISON_DATA  
**Anchor:** Production-scale pre-Transformer recipe: deep stacked LSTMs (8+8), residuals, 1st-layer bi-encoder, attention wiring for parallelism, wordpieces, beam search w/ length norm + coverage penalty, quantized inference.

## Key Content
- **Seq2seq factorization (Sec. 3):** For source \(X=(x_1,\dots,x_M)\), target \(Y=(y_1,\dots,y_N)\):  
  \[
  P(Y\mid X)=\prod_{i=1}^{N} P(y_i \mid y_{<i}; x_{1:M})
  \]
  Decoder generates until **EOS**; attention lets decoder focus on encoder states.
- **Architecture defaults (Sec. 8):** **8-layer encoder + 8-layer decoder** LSTMs; bottom encoder layer is **bidirectional** (so “9 logical LSTM passes” in encoder). **1024** LSTM units per layer. Attention: 1-hidden-layer FFN with **1024** hidden units.
- **Residual connections (Sec. 3.1):** Added between stacked LSTM layers to enable training beyond ~6 layers; authors report plain stacking works well up to **4**, barely **6**, and “very poorly beyond **8**” without residuals (vanishing/exploding gradients).
- **Attention wiring for parallelism (Abstract/Sec. 3):** Attention connects **bottom decoder layer → top encoder layer** to improve training parallelism.
- **Wordpieces for rare words (Sec. 4.1):** Shared source/target WPM; typical vocab **8k–32k** wordpieces; deterministic segmentation; helps copying names/numbers.
- **Beam search scoring (Decoder section):**  
  Length norm: \(lp(Y)=\frac{(5+|Y|)^\alpha}{(5+1)^\alpha}\).  
  Coverage penalty: \(cp(X;Y)=\beta\sum_{i=1}^{|X|}\log(\min(\sum_{j=1}^{|Y|}p_{i,j},1.0))\).  
  Final: \(s(Y,X)=\log P(Y|X)/lp(Y)+cp(X;Y)\).
- **Training pipeline (Experiments slide text):** 12 async replicas; init params \([-0.04,0.04]\); grad clip **5.0**; batch **128**; Adam lr **0.0002** for **60k** steps → SGD lr **0.5**; start halving after **1.2M** steps; dropout in ML phase only.
- **Key results (Sec. 1):** WMT’14 En→Fr **38.95 BLEU**; WMT’14 En→De **24.17 BLEU**. Human eval: ~**60%** fewer errors vs Google PBMT on several major pairs.

## When to surface
Use when students ask how classic encoder–decoder models addressed the **context-vector bottleneck/long-range dependencies** before Transformers, or want concrete **beam search/coverage** formulas and **deep LSTM + residual** design choices with real hyperparameters and BLEU numbers.
