# Card: RNN Encoder–Decoder MLE objective (Cho et al., 2014)
**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.1078  
**Role:** paper | **Need:** FORMULA_SOURCE  
**Anchor:** Maximum-likelihood training for encoder–decoder with conditional factorization \(p(\mathbf{y}\mid \mathbf{x})=\prod_t p(y_t\mid y_{<t},\mathbf{x})\)

## Key Content
- **Sequence probability factorization (Eq. 3, Sec. 2.1):** For a sequence \(\mathbf{x}=(x_1,\dots,x_T)\),
  \[
  p(\mathbf{x})=\prod_{t=1}^{T} p(x_t \mid x_{t-1},\dots,x_1).
  \]
- **Conditional seq2seq modeling (Sec. 2.2):** Model conditional distribution over variable-length sequences:
  \[
  p(y_1,\dots,y_{T'} \mid x_1,\dots,x_T).
  \]
  Encoder reads \(\mathbf{x}\) and produces a fixed-length **context/summary vector** \(c\) (final hidden state after end-of-sequence).
- **Decoder recurrence with context (Sec. 2.2):**
  \[
  h^{\langle t\rangle}=f\!\left(h^{\langle t-1\rangle},\, y_{t-1},\, c\right),
  \]
  and outputs a valid probability distribution over next token (via softmax).
- **Training objective = conditional log-likelihood (Eq. 4):**
  \[
  \max_{\theta}\ \frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^{N}\log p_{\theta}(\mathbf{y}^{(n)}\mid \mathbf{x}^{(n)}),
  \]
  where \(\theta\) are all encoder+decoder parameters; optimized with gradient-based methods (backprop through differentiable decoder outputs). Equivalent to minimizing negative log-likelihood / cross-entropy under teacher forcing (conditioning on previous gold \(y_{t-1}\)).
- **Design rationale (Sec. 3.1):** When training on phrase pairs for SMT scoring, they **ignore phrase-pair frequencies** to (1) reduce sampling cost and (2) prevent the model from merely learning corpus occurrence counts; capacity is pushed toward linguistic regularities (plausible vs. implausible translations).
- **Defaults/parameters (Experiments):**
  - Vocab limited to **15,000** most frequent words per language; ~**93%** coverage; OOV \(\rightarrow\) **[UNK]**.
  - Phrase/context vector \(c\): **1000-dimensional**.
  - Word embedding dimension: **100** (“rank-100 matrices”).
  - Weight init: Gaussian std **0.01** (recurrent matrices initialized via left singular vectors after Gaussian sampling).
  - Optimizer: **Adadelta** with \(\epsilon=10^{-6}\) (and SGD mentioned).

## When to surface
Use when students ask how seq2seq defines \(p(\mathbf{y}\mid\mathbf{x})\), why the loss is cross-entropy/NLL, how teacher forcing fits the factorization, or what “context vector bottleneck” means (fixed-length \(c\)).
