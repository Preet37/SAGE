# Card: Scheduled Sampling (bridging teacher forcing ↔ inference)
**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03099  
**Role:** paper | **Need:** FORMULA_SOURCE  
**Anchor:** Algorithm 1 scheduled sampling procedure + sampling schedules (linear/exponential/inverse-sigmoid) + token-level NLL under mixed inputs

## Key Content
- **Seq2seq/RNN likelihood factorization (teacher forcing baseline):**  
  **Eq. (1)**: \(\log P(Y\mid X)=\sum_{t=1}^{T}\log P(y_t\mid y_{1:t-1},X)\).  
  **Eq. (2)**: \(\log P(y_t\mid y_{1:t-1},X;\theta)=\log P(y_t\mid h_t;\theta)\).  
  **Eq. (3)** state update:  
  \(h_t=\begin{cases} f(X;\theta) & t=1\\ f(h_{t-1},y_{t-1};\theta) & t>1\end{cases}\).  
  Softmax over linear projection of \(h_t\) gives \(P(y_t\mid h_t;\theta)\). Add special token **\<EOS\>**; inference stops when generated.
- **Scheduled Sampling procedure (Section 2.4; Algorithm 1 concept):**  
  During training, for each token position \(t\) in mini-batch \(i\), **flip a coin**: feed previous input token as **ground truth** \(y_{t-1}\) with probability \(\epsilon_i\), else feed **model token** \(\hat y_{t-1}\) with probability \(1-\epsilon_i\).  
  \(\hat y_{t-1}\) obtained either by **sampling** from \(P(y_{t-1}\mid h_{t-1})\) or **argmax**. Coin flip **per token** worked better than per sequence (per-sequence “much worse”).
- **Schedules for \(\epsilon_i\) (Figure 2):**  
  Linear: \(\epsilon_i=\max(\epsilon,\;k-ci)\) (min truth \(\epsilon\), offset \(k\), slope \(c\)).  
  Exponential: \(\epsilon_i=k^i\) with \(k<1\).  
  Inverse sigmoid: \(\epsilon_i=\frac{k}{k+\exp(i/k)}\) with \(k\ge 1\).
- **Rationale:** reduce **exposure bias** / train–test mismatch; gradually move from easy (teacher forcing, \(\epsilon_i=1\)) to inference-like (\(\epsilon_i\to 0\)) so model learns to recover from its own mistakes.
- **Empirical notes (qualitative from excerpt):** “Always Sampling” (\(\epsilon_i=0\) throughout) performs **very poorly**; scheduled sampling improves decoding metrics even if next-step prediction may be worse.

## When to surface
Use when students ask about **teacher forcing vs inference mismatch**, **exposure bias**, or want the **exact scheduled sampling algorithm and decay formulas** for mixing ground-truth and model-generated previous tokens.
